// 01 — ABOUT

Background & Goals

Who I am, where I came from, and where I'm headed.

My Journey

I graduated with an MSc in Cyber Security, where I developed a deep interest in cloud-native security architectures — particularly the intersection of DevOps velocity and security assurance. My dissertation focused on identifying and mitigating security vulnerabilities in AWS CI/CD pipelines, which gave me a thorough understanding of how insecure pipelines can become an attacker's fastest path into a production environment.

Before and during my studies, I worked at Amazon UK, which gave me an operational perspective on large-scale infrastructure and the real-world pressures that cause teams to deprioritise security. I also completed a cybersecurity internship at Luminar Technolab, where I worked on vulnerability assessments, network analysis, and security tooling. Together, these experiences shaped how I think about security: not as a checkpoint, but as a continuous engineering practice.

My hands-on work with AWS services — including IAM, S3, EC2, CloudTrail, Security Hub, and VPC configuration — has given me practical insight into where cloud environments go wrong and how to fix them systematically, rather than reactively.

Career Goals

I'm actively seeking a role as a Cloud Security Engineer or Security Operations Analyst where I can apply my technical skills to protect cloud infrastructure at scale. I want to work in an environment that values security engineering over compliance checkbox-ticking — where threat modelling, automated detection, and secure-by-default design are the standard.

Longer term, I aim to develop deep expertise in cloud-native security tooling, threat intelligence, and secure architecture design — working toward roles that bridge the gap between engineering teams and security outcomes.
